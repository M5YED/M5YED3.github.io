Note for Observers

This page will show you some of the types of services we provide as well as some types of systems that you may be interested in.
Note: The examples of systems that we have listed below are only a few examples and we are not limited to only creating systems with the features mentioned.

Context

Before you purchase a system, you need to understand the benefits of investing in a system. The systems we provide are Automation systems which are designed to automate processes effectively.

System's are built to:
-Complete tasks faster with minimal manual intervention
-Maintain accuracy and consistency, minimizing costly mistakes
-Allow employees to focus on strategic tasks rather than repetitive, time-consuming processes
-Reduce labor and operational costs
-Handle increased workloads effortlessly as the business grows
-Ensure data is collected, processed, and stored systematically, aiding better decision-making
-Analyse records flawlessly and ensure regulatory compliance with minimal oversight
-Create faster turnaround times and streamlined processes which lead to better customer experiences
-Maximize output by working 24/7 without breaks
-Provide consistent execution of tasks, maintaining quality and reliability



Example Systems

We've listed a few examples of systems(and their funtions) that may be of interest to you. If you have any suggestions of other systems that we should list here, feel free to provide feedback to AFA by typing "I want to give feedback".

System 1: Form Submissions(Micro)
This automation gathers information from customers in the form of questions. The answers will get transfered to a documentation platform(ie. google sheets). The system can then separate answers and organise them onto seperate documents which will store the answers from previous customers. A system like this will help businesses to gather information in an organised and time-saving way.

System 2: Payment Tracking(Macro)
This automation sends out invoices and reciepts to customers using communicative platforms(ie. Gmail). The customer would then pay the amount due and, in return, recieve a reciept as their proof of purchase. The system will update its records and transfer the customer's details to a separate section of the record document that shows a log of customers who have paid for the business's service. A system like this will help businesses to collect payment in a safe and energy-efficient way.

System 3: Onboarding(Micro)
This automation sends course/marketing material to new customers in a specified order so that they get all of the relevent information they need before joining the main service. The customer would be given the information from the business's campaigns in the order that the previous customers had been given. The system can be updated to hand out different course materials to customers at certain times(ie. 3 days after the customer begins the service process). A system like this will help businesses to provide information in a non-repetitive and resource-efficient way.

System 4: Restocking Alerts(Macro)
This automation sends messages to suppliers through communicative platforms(ie. Whatsapp). The supplier would be given the stock name of the material that you need and how much of it that you require. The system can also send invoices from the supplier to a business's employee/manager for a manual confirmation of the payment transaction. A system like this will help businesses to restock materials in a hassle-free and well-prepared way.

System 5: Social Media Content Scheduling(Micro)
This automation posts pre-made content on social media platforms(ie. LinkedIn) at specified times. The content can be human-generated or AI-generated. Any AI-generated content would be tailored to promote the business and their services. A system like this will help businesses to reach new customers in an energy-saving and consistant way.
